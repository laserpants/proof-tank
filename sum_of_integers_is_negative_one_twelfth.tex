We will first consider the series

$$ \lim_{N \to \infty} \sum_{n=1}^N n(-1)^{n-1} = 1 - 2 + 3 - 4 + 5 - 6 + \cdots $$

Given that the terms of this series do not approach zero, it fails the $n$-th term test for divergence. But we will ignore that for the moment and let $s = 1 - 2 + 3 - 4 + \cdots$. Then

$$
\begin{tabular}{l@{ } l@{ } l@{ } l@{ } l}
  $ 4s $ & $ = \ s $ & $ + \ s $ & $ + \ s $ & $ + \ s $ \\
         & $ = (1 - 2 + 3 - 4 + \cdots) $ & $ + \ (1 - 2 + 3 - 4 + \cdots) $ 
         & $ + \ (1 - 2 + 3 - 4 + \cdots) $ & $ + \ (1 - 2 + 3 - 4 + \cdots) $ \\
         & $ = (1 - 2 + 3 - 4 + \cdots) $ & $ + \ 1 + (-2 + 3 -4 + 5 - \cdots) $ 
         & $ + \ 1 + (-2 + 3 -4 + 5 - \cdots) $ & $ + \ (1 - 2) + (3 - 4 + 5 - 6 + \cdots) $ \\
         & $ = (1 - 2 + 3 - 4 + \cdots) $ & $ + \ 1 + (-2 + 3 -4 + 5 - \cdots) $ 
         & $ + \ 1 + (-2 + 3 -4 + 5 - \cdots) $ & $ - \ 1 + (3 - 4 + 5 - 6 + \cdots) $ \\
         & $ = 1 + (1 - 2 + 3 - 4 + \cdots) $ & $ + \ (-2 + 3 -4 + 5 - \cdots) $ 
         & $ + \ (-2 + 3 -4 + 5 - \cdots) $ & $ + \ (3 - 4 + 5 - 6 + \cdots) $ \\
         & $ = 1 + [ \ (1 - 2 - 2 + 3) $ & $ + \ (-2 + 3 + 3 - 4) $ 
         & $ + \ (3 - 4 - 4 + 5) $ & $ + \ (-4 + 5 + 5 - 6) + \cdots \ ] $ \\
         & $ = 1 + [ \ 0 $ & $ + \ 0 $ 
         & $ + \ 0 $ & $ + \ 0 + \cdots \ ] $ \\
         & $ = 1 $
\end{tabular}
$$

This suggests that $1 - 2 + 3 - 4 + \cdots = \frac{1}{4}$. Can we transform this to the series $1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + \cdots$?

$$
\begin{tabular}{l l l l l l l l l}
      & $1$ & $+ \ 2$ & $+ \ 3$ & $+ \ 4$ & $+ \ 5$ & $+ \ 6$  & $+ \ \cdots$ \\
  $-$ & $1$ & $- \ 2$ & $+ \ 3$ & $- \ 4$ & $+ \ 5$ & $- \ 6$  & $- \ \cdots$ \\
      & $0$ & $+ \ 4$ & $+ \ 0$ & $+ \ 8$ & $+ \ 0$ & $+ \ 12$ & $+ \ \cdots$ \\
\end{tabular}
$$
